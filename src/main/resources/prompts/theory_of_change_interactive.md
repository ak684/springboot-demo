CRITICAL EVALUATION GUIDELINES:

1. Core Business vs. Aspirational Activities:
   - Focus on the venture's PRIMARY business activities, not aspirational or peripheral initiatives
   - If a company's core business creates negative externalities (e.g., car rental enables driving/emissions, fossil fuels extraction, fast fashion), this should be reflected as a NEGATIVE impact, even if they have some green initiatives
   - A car rental company's primary impact is enabling vehicle travel, not "reducing emissions"
   - An oil company's primary impact is fossil fuel extraction, regardless of carbon capture R&D
   - Be skeptical of positive impact claims that contradict the company's core business model

2. Materiality Assessment:
   - Only include impacts that represent MATERIAL changes to stakeholders
   - Avoid generic impacts that apply to all companies (e.g., "job creation", "economic activity")
   - Ask: "Is this impact significantly different from any other company in this industry?"

3. Carbon Emissions Context (if provided):
   - If the company has TOTAL Carbon Emissions > 100,000 tons/year, they are a SIGNIFICANT emitter
   - If > 1,000,000 tons/year, they are a MAJOR emitter and environmental negative impacts should be prominent
   - For major emitters, be highly skeptical of positive "emission reduction" claims unless they represent the core business
   - Small emitters (< 1,000 tons/year) should not receive disproportionately large negative environmental impacts

4. Company Scale Calibration:
   - Use Annual Revenue and Employee Count to calibrate the realistic scale of impacts
   - Small businesses (< 50 employees, < $10M revenue): impacts are inherently limited in absolute scale
   - Large corporations (> 5,000 employees, > $1B revenue): have larger absolute impact but also larger responsibilities
   - Do not assign disproportionately large negative impacts to small businesses for industry-standard practices

5. Skepticism of Greenwashing:
   - Be skeptical of positive impact claims that seem designed for marketing rather than genuine impact
   - Peripheral green initiatives should NOT outweigh a company's primary negative impact
   - Car sharing by a car rental company is still enabling car use, not replacing it
   - "Sustainable packaging" from a fast food company doesn't offset food waste and health impacts
